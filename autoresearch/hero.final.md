# Marketing Hero — final (3 variants)

All three variants score **9/10** on the 10-point rubric. The unreachable point at ≤12 words is PROOF (photo + temple-stamped receipt) — that claim lives in the body copy and ASC description. Tradeoff documented in LOG.md round 3.

## Foundation — likhita.org

> "Likhita Foundation: koti by your hand, deposited at Bhadrachalam or Varanasi, at-cost."

12 words. Names: Likhita Foundation. Sanskrit: koti. Skepticism: "by your hand." Temple: Bhadrachalam, Varanasi. Non-profit: "at-cost." Physical: "deposited."

12 words. Names: Likhita Foundation. Sanskrit: sankalpam, koti. Skepticism: "by your hand." Temple: Bhadrachalam. Non-profit: "at-cost." Physical: implied by "to Bhadrachalam."

## Likhita Ram — likhitaram.org

> "Likhita Foundation: Ram Naam Lekhan, your hand, deposited at Varanasi, at-cost."

12 words. Names: Likhita Foundation. Sanskrit: naam, lekhan. Skepticism: "your hand." Temple: Varanasi (Ram Naam Bank). Non-profit: "at-cost." Physical: "deposited."

## Why each hero takes a slightly different shape

- **Foundation site** is the meta-page across both apps — it leads with the foundation name (because that is the page's subject), then unifies the two destinations.
- **Likhita Rama** site sells the Telugu pilgrimage. It opens with "Sankalpam to Bhadrachalam" — destination-led, ritual-led — then names the foundation as the steward.
- **Likhita Ram** site sells the Hindi pilgrimage to a Varanasi public bank. The foundation leads (because it's the trust signal that distinguishes from sect-tied competitors), then the practice and the destination.

## Tradeoff note (carry forward to body copy)

PROOF (photo + temple-stamped receipt back) is the single rubric item we could not fit in 12 words alongside the rest. The body copy under the hero, and the ASC descriptions, must therefore lead the second-line subhead with the proof claim. Recommended subhead pattern across all three sites:

> "A photograph of your book at the temple, and a temple-stamped receipt, returned to you within 60 days."

That single sentence picks up the missing rubric point and lands the differentiator that no competitor can match.
